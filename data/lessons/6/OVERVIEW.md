# Chapter 6: My World

This chapter explores the personal world of the learner, covering possession, family relationships,
origins, and the nuances of social interaction.

## Lesson 6.1: To Have & Not Have (jau5, mou5)

- **Concepts:** Expressing existence and possession with `jau5` (have) and `mou5` (not have). Asking
  "Do you have...?" (`jau5 mou5`).
- **Examples:**
  - `jau5` (жңү) - Have
  - `mou5` (еҶҮ) - Not have
  - `jau5 mou5?` (жңүеҶҮпјҹ) - Have or not have?
  - `jau5 syu1` (жңүжӣё) - Have a book (Recycled from Ch 4)
  - `mou5 maau1` (еҶҮиІ“) - Not have a cat (Recycled from Ch 4)
- **Dialogues:**
  - **Dialogue 1 (Formal - Personal Items):**
    - A: `nei5 jau5 mou5 syu1 aa3?` (дҪ жңүеҶҮжӣёе‘Җпјҹ - "Do you have any books?")
    - B: `jau5 aa3. ngo5 jau5 saam1 bun2 syu1.` (жңүе‘ҖгҖӮжҲ‘жңүдёүжң¬жӣёгҖӮ)
    - A: `nei5 ne1? nei5 hai6 m4 hai6 hok6 saang1 aa3?` (дҪ е‘ўпјҹдҪ дҝӮе””дҝӮеӯёз”ҹе‘Җпјҹ)
  - **Dialogue 2 (Informal - Pets):**
    - A: `wai2, nei5 jau5 mou5 maau1 aa3?` (е–ӮпјҢдҪ жңүеҶҮиІ“е‘Җпјҹ)
    - B: `mou5 wo3. ngo5 jau5 gau2.` (еҶҮе–ҺгҖӮжҲ‘жңүзӢ—гҖӮ)
- **Exercises:**
  - `6.1.2` (Reading): `jau5, mou5`
  - `6.1.3` (Reading): `jau5 mou5?`
  - `6.1.7` (Reading): `иІ“пјҢжӣё`
  - `6.1.8` (Reading): `дҪ жңүеҶҮе‘Җпјҹ`
  - `6.1.4` (Unscramble): `ngo5 jau5 saam1 bun2 syu1.`
  - `6.1.5` (Unscramble): `ngo5 mou5 maau1.`
  - `6.1.6` (Unscramble): `nei5 jau5 mou5 syu1 aa3?`

## Lesson 6.2: Family Members (uk1 kei2 jan4)

- **Concepts:** Vocabulary for family members. Using "to have" with family and classifiers.
- **Examples:**
  - `ba4 ba1` (зҲёзҲё) - Dad
  - `ma4 ma1` (еӘҪеӘҪ) - Mom
  - `go4 go1` (е“Ҙе“Ҙ) - Older brother
  - `mui4 mui2` (еҰ№еҰ№) - Younger sister
  - `loeng5 go3 go4 go1` (е…©еҖӢе“Ҙе“Ҙ) - Two older brothers
  - `uk1 kei2 jan4` (еұӢдјҒдәә) - Family members
- **Dialogues:**
  - **Dialogue 1 (Formal - Family Size):**
    - A: `nei5 jau5 mou5 mui4 mui2 aa3?` (дҪ жңүеҶҮеҰ№еҰ№е‘Җпјҹ)
    - B: `mou5 aa3. ngo5 jau5 loeng5 go3 go4 go1.` (еҶҮе‘ҖгҖӮжҲ‘жңүе…©еҖӢе“Ҙе“ҘгҖӮ)
  - **Dialogue 2 (Informal - Family Well-being):**
    - A: `wai2, nei5 uk1 kei2 jan4 hou2 maa3?` (е–ӮпјҢдҪ еұӢдјҒдәәеҘҪе—Һпјҹ)
    - B: `gei2 hou2 aa3. nei5 jau5 mou5 go4 go1 aa3?` (е№ҫеҘҪе‘ҖгҖӮдҪ жңүеҶҮе“Ҙе“Ҙе‘Җпјҹ)
- **Exercises:**
  - `6.2.2` (Reading): `ba4 ba1, ma4 ma1`
  - `6.2.4` (Reading): `go4 go1, mui4 mui2`
  - `6.2.5` (Reading): `uk1 kei2 jan4`
  - `6.2.3` (Unscramble): `ngo5 jau5 loeng5 go3 go4 go1.`
  - `6.2.6` (Unscramble): `nei5 jau5 mou5 mui4 mui2 aa3?`

## Lesson 6.3: Origins & Nationalities

- **Concepts:** Asking about origins and stating nationality (`country + jan4`).
- **Examples:**
  - `bin1 dou6 jan4?` (йӮҠеәҰдәәпјҹ) - From where? (Nationality)
  - `mei5 gwok3` (зҫҺеңӢ) - USA
  - `jing1 gwok3` (иӢұеңӢ) - UK
  - `mei5 gwok3 jan4` (зҫҺеңӢдәә) - American
- **Dialogues:**
  - **Dialogue 1 (Formal):**
    - A: `nei5 hai6 bin1 dou6 jan4 aa3?` (дҪ дҝӮйӮҠеәҰдәәе‘Җпјҹ - "Where are you from?")
    - B: `ngo5 hai6 mei5 gwok3 jan4.` (жҲ‘дҝӮзҫҺеңӢдәәгҖӮ)
  - **Dialogue 2 (Informal):**
    - A: `wai2, nei5 bin1 dou6 jan4 aa3?` (е–ӮпјҢдҪ йӮҠеәҰдәәе‘Җпјҹ)
    - B: `mei5 gwok3 jan4 lo1. nei5 ne1?` (зҫҺеңӢдәәеӣүгҖӮдҪ е‘ўпјҹ)
- **Exercises:**
  - `6.3.2` (Reading): `mei5 gwok3, jing1 gwok3`
  - `6.3.4` (Reading): `bin1 dou6 jan4?`
  - `6.3.3` (Unscramble): `ngo5 hai6 mei5 gwok3 jan4.`
  - `6.3.5` (Unscramble): `nei5 hai6 bin1 dou6 jan4 aa3?`

## Lesson 6.4: Abilities & Languages (sik1)

- **Concepts:** Using `sik1` (to know how to / to understand) for languages and skills. Introduction
  of the verb `gong2` (to speak).
- **Examples:**
  - `sik1` (иӯҳ) - To know / To understand
  - `m4 sik1` (е””иӯҳ) - Not know
  - `gong2` (и¬ӣ) - To speak
  - `gwong2 dung1 waa2` (е»Јжқұи©ұ) - Cantonese
  - `jing1 man2` (иӢұж–Ү) - English
- **Dialogues:**
  - **Dialogue 1 (Formal):**
    - A: `nei5 sik1 m4 sik1 gong2 gwong2 dung1 waa2 aa3?` (дҪ иӯҳе””иӯҳи¬ӣе»Јжқұи©ұе‘Җпјҹ)
    - B: `ngo5 sik1 gong2 jing1 man2. gwong2 dung1 waa2 ngo5 m4 sik1.` (жҲ‘иӯҳи¬ӣиӢұж–ҮгҖӮе»Јжқұи©ұжҲ‘е””иӯҳгҖӮ)
  - **Dialogue 2 (Informal):**
    - A: `wai2, nei5 sik1 m4 sik1 gong2 gwong2 dung1 waa2?` (е–ӮпјҢдҪ иӯҳе””иӯҳи¬ӣе»Јжқұи©ұпјҹ)
    - B: `иӢұж–Үе°ұиӯҳгҖӮе»Јжқұи©ұе°ұе””иӯҳе–ҮгҖӮ` (English, yes. Cantonese, no.)
- **Exercises:**
  - `6.4.2` (Reading): `е»Јжқұи©ұ`
  - `6.4.4` (Reading): `иӢұж–Ү`
  - `6.4.5` (Reading): `и¬ӣпјҢиӯҳе””иӯҳпјҹ`
  - `6.4.3` (Unscramble): `ngo5 sik1 gong2 jing1 man2.`
  - `6.4.6` (Unscramble): `nei5 sik1 m4 sik1 gong2 gwong2 dung1 waa2 aa3?`

## Lesson 6.5: Conversational Flow (laa1, ne1)

- **Concepts:** Using `laa1` (suggestions) and `ne1` (continuing questions).
- **Examples:**
  - `laa1` (е•Ұ) - Suggestion particle (Let's...)
  - `ne1` (е‘ў) - Continuation particle (And...?)
  - `gong2 laa1` (и¬ӣе•Ұ) - Speak (suggestion)
- **Dialogues:**
  - **Dialogue 1 (Formal):**
    - A: `ngo5 dei6 gong2 gwong2 dung1 waa2 laa1!` (жҲ‘е“Ӣи¬ӣе»Јжқұи©ұе•ҰпјҒ - "Let's speak Cantonese!")
    - B: `hou2 aa3! ngo5 sik1 gong2, nei5 ne1?` (еҘҪе‘ҖпјҒжҲ‘иӯҳи¬ӣпјҢдҪ е‘ўпјҹ)
    - A: `ngo5 dou1 sik1.` (жҲ‘йғҪиӯҳгҖӮ)
  - **Dialogue 2 (Informal):**
    - A: `bat1 jyu4 gong2 gwong2 dung1 waa2 laa1?` (дёҚеҰӮи¬ӣе»Јжқұи©ұе•Ұпјҹ)
    - B: `hou2. ngo5 sik1 aa3, nei5 ne1?` (еҘҪгҖӮжҲ‘иӯҳе‘ҖпјҢдҪ е‘ўпјҹ)
    - A: `жҲ‘йғҪиӯҳе‘ҖпјҒ` (I also know how!)
- **Exercises:**
  - `6.5.2` (Reading): `и¬ӣе•Ұ`
  - `6.5.4` (Reading): `еҺ»е•Ұ`
  - `6.5.5` (Reading): `дҪ е‘ўпјҹ`
  - `6.5.3` (Unscramble): `жҲ‘е“Ӣи¬ӣе»Јжқұи©ұе•ҰпјҒ`
  - `6.5.6` (Unscramble): `жҲ‘иӯҳи¬ӣпјҢдҪ е‘ўпјҹ`
