# Chapter 6: My World

This chapter explores the personal world of the learner, covering possession, family relationships, origins, and the nuances of social interaction.

## Lesson 6.1: To Have & Not Have (jau5, mou5)

- **Concepts:** Expressing existence and possession with `jau5` (have) and `mou5` (not have). Asking "Do you have...?" (`jau5 mou5`).
- **Examples:**
  - `jau5` (有) - Have
  - `mou5` (冇) - Not have
  - `jau5 mou5?` (有無？) - Have or not have?
  - `jau5 syu1` (有書) - Have a book (Recycled from Ch 4)
  - `mou5 maau1` (冇貓) - Not have a cat (Recycled from Ch 4)
- **Dialogues:**
  - **Dialogue 1 (Formal - Personal Items):**
    - A: `nei5 jau5 mou5 syu1 aa3?` (你有無書呀？ - "Do you have any books?")
    - B: `jau5 aa3. ngo5 jau5 saam1 bun2 syu1.` (有呀。我有三本書。)
    - A: `nei5 ne1? nei5 hai6 m4 hai6 hok6 saang1 aa3?` (你呢？你係唔係學生呀？)
  - **Dialogue 2 (Informal - Pets):**
    - A: `wai2, nei5 jau5 mou5 maau1 aa3?` (喂，你有無貓呀？)
    - B: `mou5 wo3. ngo5 jau5 gau2.` (冇喎。我有狗。)
- **Exercises:**
  - `6.1.2` (Reading): `jau5, mou5`
  - `6.1.3` (Reading): `jau5 mou5?`
  - `6.1.7` (Reading): `貓，書`
  - `6.1.8` (Reading): `你，呀`
  - `6.1.4` (Unscramble): `ngo5 jau5 saam1 bun2 syu1.`
  - `6.1.5` (Unscramble): `ngo5 mou5 maau1.`
  - `6.1.6` (Unscramble): `nei5 jau5 mou5 syu1 aa3?`

## Lesson 6.2: Family Members (uk1 kei2 jan4)

- **Concepts:** Vocabulary for family members. Using "to have" with family and classifiers.
- **Examples:**
  - `ba4 ba1` (爸爸) - Dad
  - `ma4 ma1` (媽媽) - Mom
  - `go4 go1` (哥哥) - Older brother
  - `mui4 mui2` (妹妹) - Younger sister
  - `loeng5 go3 go4 go1` (兩個哥哥) - Two older brothers
  - `uk1 kei2 jan4` (屋企人) - Family members
- **Dialogues:**
  - **Dialogue 1 (Formal - Family Size):**
    - A: `nei5 jau5 mou5 mui4 mui2 aa3?` (你有無妹妹呀？)
    - B: `mou5 aa3. ngo5 jau5 loeng5 go3 go4 go1.` (冇呀。我有兩個哥哥。)
  - **Dialogue 2 (Informal - Family Well-being):**
    - A: `wai2, nei5 uk1 kei2 jan4 hou2 maa3?` (喂，你屋企人好嗎？)
    - B: `gei2 hou2 aa3. nei5 jau5 mou5 go4 go1 aa3?` (幾好呀。你有無哥哥呀？)
- **Exercises:**
  - `6.2.2` (Reading): `ba4 ba1, ma4 ma1`
  - `6.2.4` (Reading): `go4 go1, mui4 mui2`
  - `6.2.5` (Reading): `uk1 kei2 jan4`
  - `6.2.3` (Unscramble): `ngo5 jau5 loeng5 go3 go4 go1.`
  - `6.2.6` (Unscramble): `nei5 jau5 mou5 mui4 mui2 aa3?`

## Lesson 6.3: Origins & Nationalities

- **Concepts:** Asking about origins and stating nationality (`country + jan4`).
- **Examples:**
  - `bin1 dou6 jan4?` (邊度人？) - From where? (Nationality)
  - `mei5 gwok3` (美國) - USA
  - `jing1 gwok3` (英國) - UK
  - `mei5 gwok3 jan4` (美國人) - American
- **Dialogues:**
  - **Dialogue 1 (Formal):**
    - A: `nei5 hai6 bin1 dou6 jan4 aa3?` (你係邊度人呀？ - "Where are you from?")
    - B: `ngo5 hai6 mei5 gwok3 jan4.` (我係美國人。)
  - **Dialogue 2 (Informal):**
    - A: `wai2, nei5 bin1 dou6 jan4 aa3?` (喂，你邊度人呀？)
    - B: `mei5 gwok3 jan4 lo1. nei5 ne1?` (美國人囉。你呢？)
- **Exercises:**
  - `6.3.2` (Reading): `mei5 gwok3, jing1 gwok3`
  - `6.3.4` (Reading): `bin1 dou6 jan4?`
  - `6.3.3` (Unscramble): `ngo5 hai6 mei5 gwok3 jan4.`
  - `6.3.5` (Unscramble): `nei5 hai6 bin1 dou6 jan4 aa3?`

## Lesson 6.4: Abilities & Languages (sik1)

- **Concepts:** Using `sik1` (to know how to / to understand) for languages and skills. Introduction of the verb `gong2` (to speak).
- **Examples:**
  - `sik1` (識) - To know / To understand
  - `m4 sik1` (唔識) - Not know
  - `gong2` (講) - To speak
  - `gwong2 dung1 waa2` (廣東話) - Cantonese
  - `jing1 man2` (英文) - English
- **Dialogues:**
  - **Dialogue 1 (Formal):**
    - A: `nei5 sik1 m4 sik1 gong2 gwong2 dung1 waa2 aa3?` (你識唔識講廣東話呀？)
    - B: `ngo5 sik1 gong2 jing1 man2. gwong2 dung1 waa2 ngo5 m4 sik1.` (我識講英文。廣東話我唔識。)
  - **Dialogue 2 (Informal):**
    - A: `wai2, nei5 sik1 m4 sik1 gong2 gwong2 dung1 waa2?` (喂，你識唔識講廣東話？)
    - B: `英文就識。廣東話就唔識喇。` (English, yes. Cantonese, no.)
- **Exercises:**
  - `6.4.2` (Reading): `廣東話`
  - `6.4.4` (Reading): `英文`
  - `6.4.5` (Reading): `講，識唔識？`
  - `6.4.3` (Unscramble): `ngo5 sik1 gong2 jing1 man2.`
  - `6.4.6` (Unscramble): `nei5 sik1 m4 sik1 gong2 gwong2 dung1 waa2 aa3?`

## Lesson 6.5: Conversational Flow (laa1, ne1)

- **Concepts:** Using `laa1` (suggestions) and `ne1` (continuing questions).
- **Examples:**
  - `laa1` (啦) - Suggestion particle (Let's...)
  - `ne1` (呢) - Continuation particle (And...?)
  - `gong2 laa1` (講啦) - Speak (suggestion)
- **Dialogues:**
  - **Dialogue 1 (Formal):**
    - A: `ngo5 dei6 gong2 gwong2 dung1 waa2 laa1!` (我哋講廣東話啦！ - "Let's speak Cantonese!")
    - B: `hou2 aa3! ngo5 sik1 gong2, nei5 ne1?` (好呀！我識講，你呢？)
    - A: `ngo5 dou1 sik1.` (我都識。)
  - **Dialogue 2 (Informal):**
    - A: `bat1 jyu4 gong2 gwong2 dung1 waa2 laa1?` (不如講廣東話啦？)
    - B: `hou2. ngo5 sik1 aa3, nei5 ne1?` (好。我識呀，你呢？)
    - A: `我都識呀！` (I also know how!)
- **Exercises:**
  - `6.5.2` (Reading): `講啦`
  - `6.5.4` (Reading): `去啦`
  - `6.5.5` (Reading): `你呢？`
  - `6.5.3` (Unscramble): `我哋講廣東話啦！`
  - `6.5.6` (Unscramble): `我識講，你呢？`
