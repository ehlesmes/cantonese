# Chapter 3: Small Talk & Pronouns

This chapter expands on basic greetings by introducing essential pronouns, yes/no question structures, and topics for small talk like professions and hobbies.

## Lesson 3.1: Expanding Pronouns

_Learning how to refer to others and groups._

- **Concepts:** Formalizing "He/She/It" (`keoi5`) and introducing the plural marker `dei6` ("We", "You all", "They").
- **Examples:**
  - `keoi5` (佢) - He / She / It
  - `ngo5 dei6` (我哋) - We
  - `nei5 dei6` (你哋) - You all
  - `keoi5 dei6` (佢哋) - They
- **Dialogues:**
  - **Dialogue 1 (Formal):**
    - A: `can4 sin1 saang1, nei5 dei6 hou2.` (陳先生，你哋好。 - "Mr. Chan, hello to you all.")
    - B: `nei5 hou2. keoi5 hai6 wong4 siu2 ze2.` (你好。佢係黃小姐。)
  - **Dialogue 2 (Colloquial):**
    - A: `keoi5 dei6 heoi3 bin1 aa3?` (佢哋去邊呀？)
    - B: `keoi5 dei6 heoi3 sik6 faan6.` (佢哋去食飯。)
- **Exercises:**
  - `3.1.2` (Reading): `你哋、我哋`
  - `3.1.3` (Unscramble): `佢哋去邊呀？`
  - `3.1.4` (Reading): `佢係`
  - `3.1.5` (Unscramble): `佢哋去食飯`

## Lesson 3.2: Asking Yes/No Questions

_The essential "A-not-A" question structure._

- **Concepts:** The "A-not-A" structure (Verb + m4 + Verb) for asking yes/no questions. Using the `maa3` particle for formal yes/no questions.
- **Examples:**
  - `hai6 m4 hai6` (係唔係) - Is it? / Yes or no?
  - `sik6 m4 sik6` (食唔食) - Eat or not eat?
  - `hou2 maa3` (好嗎) - Good? (Formal yes/no)
- **Dialogues:**
  - **Dialogue 1 (A-not-A):**
    - A: `nei5 sik6 m4 sik6 faan6 aa3?` (你食唔食飯呀？)
    - B: `ngo5 sik6. nei5 ne1?` (我食。你呢？)
  - **Dialogue 2 (maa3 particle):**
    - A: `nei5 hou2 maa3?` (你好嗎？)
    - B: `ngo5 hou2 hou2.` (我好好。)
- **Exercises:**
  - `3.2.2` (Reading): `係唔係`
  - `3.2.3` (Unscramble): `你食唔食飯呀？`
  - `3.2.4` (Reading): `你食飯好嗎？`
  - `3.2.5` (Unscramble): `我唔食喇`

## Lesson 3.3: Professions

_Talking about what you do for work._

- **Concepts:** Asking "What do you do?" and stating common professions.
- **Examples:**
  - `zou6 me1 aa3` (做咩呀) - Do what?
  - `zou6 je5` (做嘢) - Work / Do things
  - `hok6 saang1` (學生) - Student
  - `sin1 saang1` (先生) - Teacher (also Mr.)
- **Dialogues:**
  - **Dialogue 1 (Informal Work Inquiry):**
    - A: `nei5 zou6 me1 aa3?` (你做咩呀？ - "What do you do?")
    - B: `ngo5 hai6 hok6 saang1.` (我係學生。)
  - **Dialogue 2 (Formal Work Inquiry):**
    - A: `wong4 siu2 ze2, nei5 zou6 me1 je5 aa3?` (黃小姐，你做咩嘢呀？)
    - B: `ngo5 hai6 sin1 saang1.` (我係先生。)
- **Exercises:**
  - `3.3.2` (Reading): `做咩呀？`
  - `3.3.3` (Unscramble): `我係學生`
  - `3.3.4` (Reading): `學生、先生`
  - `3.3.5` (Unscramble): `你好，先生`

## Lesson 3.4: Hobbies

_Discussing free-time activities._

- **Concepts:** Asking "What do you like to do?" and common hobbies.
- **Examples:**
  - `zung1 ji3` (鍾意) - Like
  - `tai2 syu1` (睇書) - Read books
  - `teng1 go1` (聽歌) - Listen to songs
- **Dialogues:**
  - **Dialogue 1 (Informal - Hobbies):**
    - A: `nei5 zung1 ji3 zou6 me1 aa3?` (你鍾意做咩呀？)
    - B: `ngo5 zung1 ji3 tai2 syu1. nei5 ne1?` (我鍾意睇書。你呢？)
    - A: `ngo5 zung1 ji3 teng1 go1.` (我鍾意聽歌。)
  - **Dialogue 2 (Formal - Inquiring Hobbies):**
    - A: `wong4 sin1 saang1, ceng2 man6 nei5 ping4 si4 jau5 me1 si3 hou3 ni1?` (黃先生，請問你平時有咩嗜好呢？)
    - B: `ngo5 bei2 gaau3 zung1 ji3 leoi5 jau4.` (我比較鍾意旅遊。)
    - A: `ming4 baak6, do1 ze6 fan2 hoeng2.` (明白，多謝分享。)
- **Exercises:**
  - `3.4.2` (Reading): `睇書` (Covers: 睇, 書)
  - `3.4.6` (Reading): `嗜好` (Covers: 嗜, 好)
  - `3.4.7` (Reading): `旅遊` (Covers: 旅, 遊)
  - `3.4.3` (Unscramble): `我鍾意聽歌` (Covers: 我, 鍾, 意, 聽, 歌)
  - `3.4.4` (Reading): `鍾意` (Covers: 鍾, 意)
  - `3.4.5` (Unscramble): `你鍾意做咩呀？` (Covers: 你, 鍾, 意, 做, 咩, 呀)

## Lesson 3.5: Softening Particles (aa3, laa3)

_Learning how to change the mood of a sentence._

- **Concepts:** Focusing on `aa3/aa1` (softening/questions) and `laa3/laa1` (already/suggestions).
- **Examples:**
  - `aa3` (呀) - Softens a sentence or ends an open question.
  - `laa3` (喇) - Indicates a change of state (already).
- **Dialogues:**
  - **Dialogue 1 (Informal - Usage):**
    - A: `ngo5 heoi3 sik6 faan6 laa3.` (我去食飯喇。)
    - B: `hou2 aa3. nei5 heoi3 bin1 aa3?` (好呀。你去邊呀？)
    - A: `ngo5 heoi3 sik6 faan6.` (我去食飯。)
  - **Dialogue 2 (Formal - Request):**
    - A: `can4 ging1 lei5, maa4 faan4 bong1 ngo5 cin1 go3 ming4.` (陳經理，麻煩幫我簽個名。)
    - B: `hou2 ge3, cin1 hai2 ni1 dou6 hai6 mai6?` (好嘅，簽喺呢度係咪？)
    - A: `hai6, m4 goi1 saai3.` (係，唔該晒。)
- **Exercises:**
  - `3.5.2` (Reading): `好呀` (Covers: 好, 呀)
  - `3.5.6` (Reading): `經理` (Covers: 經, 理)
  - `3.5.7` (Reading): `麻煩` (Covers: 麻煩)
  - `3.5.8` (Reading): `簽名` (Covers: 簽, 名)
  - `3.5.3` (Unscramble): `我去食飯喇` (Covers: 我, 去, 食, 飯, 喇)

## Lesson 3.6: Possession & Attributes (ge3)

_The essential structural particle for 'belonging'._

- **Concepts:** The possessive marker `ge3` (嘅).
- **Examples:**
  - `ge3` (嘅) - Possession (my/your) or describing things.
  - `ngo5 ge3` (我嘅) - Mine.
- **Dialogues:**
  - **Dialogue 1 (Informal - Possession):**
    - A: `keoi5 hai6 m4 hai6 nei5 ge3 sin1 saang1 aa3?` (佢係唔係你嘅先生呀？)
    - B: `m4 hai6. hai6 keoi5 ge3 sin1 saang1.` (唔係。係佢嘅先生。)
    - A: `o4, hou2 aa3.` (哦，好呀。)
  - **Dialogue 2 (Formal - Identifying Belongings):**
    - A: `ni1 fan6 man4 gin2 hai6 mai6 can4 ging1 lei5 ge3?` (呢份文件係咪陳經理嘅？)
    - B: `hai6, hai6 keoi5 ge3.` (係，係佢嘅。)
- **Exercises:**
  - `3.6.2` (Reading): `我嘅` (Covers: 我, 嘅)
  - `3.6.6` (Reading): `文件` (Covers: 文, 件)
  - `3.6.3` (Unscramble): `佢係我嘅先生` (Covers: 佢, 係, 我, 嘅, 先, 生)
  - `3.6.4` (Reading): `你嘅` (Covers: 你, 嘅)
  - `3.6.5` (Unscramble): `佢哋係你嘅先生嗎？` (Covers: 佢, 哋, 係, 你, 嘅, 先, 生, 嗎)
