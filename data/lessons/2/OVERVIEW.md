# Chapter 2: Greetings, Introductions & Small Talk

This chapter covers the essentials of starting and ending conversations in both formal and
colloquial settings.

## Lesson 2.1: Hello and "What's Up?"

_Balancing formal greetings with daily colloquialisms._

- **Concepts:** Formal `你好` vs. Spontaneous `點呀?`. Greetings for different times of day.
- **Examples:**
  - `nei5 hou2` (你好) - Hello (Formal)
  - `dim2 aa3?` (點呀？) - What's up? (Colloquial)
  - `zou2 san1` (早晨) - Good morning
  - `ng5 on1` (午安) - Good afternoon (Formal)
  - `maan5 on1` (晚安) - Good evening (Formal)
  - `haa1 lo3` (哈囉) - Hello (Colloquial)
- **Dialogues:**
  - **Dialogue 1 (Formal Morning):**
    - A: `zou2 san1, wong4 sin1 saang1.` (早晨，黃先生。)
    - B: `zou2 san1, nei5 hou2 maa3?` (早晨，你好嗎？)
    - A: `ngo5 hou2 hou2, jau6 lau4 nei5?` (我好好，有勞你？ - "I'm fine, thanks for asking")
  - **Dialogue 2 (Colloquial):**
    - A: `haa1 lo3! dim2 aa3?` (哈囉！點呀？)
    - B: `gei2 hou2 aa3, nei5 ne1?` (幾好呀，你呢？)
    - A: `ngo5 dou1 hai6 gam2 laa1.` (我都係噉啦。 - "I'm just the same / so-so")
- **Exercises:**
  - `2.1.2` (Reading): `你好` (Covers: 你, 好)
  - `2.1.3` (Unscramble): `你好！點呀？` (Covers: 你, 好, 點, 呀)
  - `2.1.4` (Reading): `早晨` (Covers: 早, 晨)
  - `2.1.6` (Reading): `晚安` (Covers: 晚, 安)
  - `2.1.7` (Reading): `午安` (Covers: 午, 安)
  - `2.1.8` (Unscramble): `哈囉！早晨。` (Covers: 哈, 囉, 早, 晨)

## Lesson 2.2: Who are You?

_Self-introductions and identity._

- **Concepts:** `我係` (I am) vs `我叫` (I am called). Expressing pleasure in meeting. Common name
  prefixes (`阿`).
- **Examples:**
  - `ngo5 hai6` (我係) - I am
  - `ngo5 giu3` (我叫) - My name is
  - `nei5 giu3 me1 meng2?` (你叫咩名？) - What is your name?
  - `aa3 ming4` (阿明) - Ah Ming (Common name format)
  - `hou2 gou1 hing3 sik1 dou2 nei5` (好高興識到你) - Nice to meet you
- **Dialogues:**
  - **Dialogue 1 (Basic Intro):**
    - A: `nei5 hou2! 我叫阿明。` (你好！我叫阿明。)
    - B: `阿明，你好。我係阿玲。` (阿明，你好。我係阿玲。)
  - **Dialogue 2 (Inquiring Name):**
    - A: `你好！你叫咩名呀？` (你好！你叫咩名呀？)
    - B: `我叫阿強。你呢？` (我叫阿強。你呢？)
    - A: `我係阿芬。好高興識到你。` (我係阿芬。好高興識到你。)
- **Exercises:**
  - `2.2.6` (Reading): `我係` (Covers: 我, 係)
  - `2.2.10` (Reading): `我叫` (Covers: 我, 叫)
  - `2.2.3` (Unscramble): `你叫咩名？` (Covers: 你, 叫, 咩, 名)
  - `2.2.5` (Reading): `你叫咩名？` (Covers: 你, 叫, 咩, 名)
  - `2.2.2` (Reading): `阿明` (Covers: 阿, 明)
  - `2.2.4` (Reading): `好高興` (Covers: 好, 高, 興)
  - `2.2.7` (Reading): `識到你` (Covers: 識, 到, 你)
  - `2.2.9` (Unscramble): `我叫阿明` (Covers: 我, 叫, 阿, 明)
  - `2.2.8` (Unscramble): `好高興識到你` (Covers: 好, 高, 興, 識, 到, 你)

## Lesson 2.3: The Cultural "Small Talk"

_Introducing the most common Cantonese colloquialisms._

- **Concepts:** Asking about eating as a greeting (`食咗飯未？`), "Where are you going?"
  (`去邊呀？`), and return questions (`你呢？`).
- **Examples:**
  - `sik6 zo2 faan6 mei6?` (食咗飯未？) - Have you eaten yet? (Common greeting)
  - `sik6 zo2 laa3` (食咗喇) - Eaten already
  - `nei5 ne1?` (你呢？) - And you?
  - `heoi3 bin1 aa3?` (去邊呀？) - Where are you going?
- **Dialogues:**
  - **Dialogue 1 (Formal - Busy Inquiry):**
    - A: `can4 sin1 saang1, nei5 hou2. nei5 mong4 m4 mong4 aa3?` (陳先生，你好。你忙唔忙呀？)
    - B: `m4 hai6 hou2 mong4. nei5 ne1?` (唔係好忙。你呢？)
    - A: `ngo5 dou1 m4 hai6 hou2 mong4.` (我都唔係好忙。)
  - **Dialogue 2 (Informal - Eating):**
    - A: `aa3 ling4! sik6 zo2 faan6 mei6 aa3?` (阿玲！食咗飯未呀？)
    - B: `sik6 zo2 laa3, nei5 ne1?` (食咗喇，你呢？)
    - A: `ngo5 mei6 aa3. ngo5 ji4 gaa1 heoi3 sik6 faan6.` (我未呀。我宜家去食飯。)
- **Exercises:**
  - `2.3.6` (Reading): `去邊呀？` (Covers: 去, 邊, 呀)
  - `2.3.7` (Reading): `忙唔忙呀？` (Covers: 忙, 唔, 呀)
  - `2.3.3` (Unscramble): `食咗喇，你呢？` (Covers: 食, 咗, 喇, 你, 呢)
  - `2.3.4` (Reading): `食咗喇` (Covers: 食, 咗, 喇)
  - `2.3.5` (Reading): `你呢？` (Covers: 你, 呢)
  - `2.3.6` (Reading): `去邊呀？` (Covers: 去, 邊, 呀)

## Lesson 2.4: Presenting Others

_Formal and informal introductions of third parties._

- **Concepts:** `呢位係` (This is) and more formal phrases.
- **Examples:**
  - `ni1 wai2 hai6` (呢位係) - This (person) is...
  - `hou2 wing4 hang6 sik1 dou2 nei5` (好榮幸識到你) - Very honored to meet you (Formal)
- **Dialogues:**
  - **Dialogue 1 (Formal - Presenting Others):**
    - A: `can4 sin1 saang1, ni1 wai2 hai6 wong4 siu2 ze2.` (陳先生，呢位係黃小姐。)
    - B: `wong4 siu2 ze2, nei5 hou2. hou2 wing4 hang6 sik1 dou2 nei5.`
      (黃小姐，你好。好榮幸識到你。)
    - C: `can4 sin1 saang1, nei5 taai3 haak3 hei3 laa3.` (陳先生，你太客氣喇。)
  - **Dialogue 2 (Informal - Introducing Friends):**
    - A: `wai2 aa3 ming4, ni1 go3 hai6 ngo5 pang4 jau5 aa3 koeng4.` (喂阿明，呢個係我朋友阿強。)
    - B: `nei5 hou2! aa3 koeng4, nei5 hou2 maa3?` (你好！阿強，你好嗎？)
    - C: `gei2 hou2 aa3, do1 ze6.` (幾好呀，多謝。)
- **Exercises:**
  - `2.4.2` (Reading): `呢位係黃小姐` (Covers: 呢, 位, 係, 黃, 小, 姐)
  - `2.4.8` (Reading): `朋友` (Covers: 朋, 友)
  - `2.4.3` (Unscramble): `好榮幸識到你` (Covers: 好, 榮, 幸, 識, 到, 你)
  - `2.4.4` (Reading): `好榮幸` (Covers: 好, 榮, 幸)
  - `2.4.5` (Reading): `識到你` (Covers: 識, 到, 你)

## Lesson 2.5: See You Later & "Yum Cha"

_Closing a conversation and making future plans._

- **Concepts:** Goodbye, "See you tomorrow", and colloquial closings.
- **Examples:**
  - `zoi3 gin3` (再見) - Goodbye (Standard/Formal)
  - `ting1 jat6 gin3` (聽日見) - See you tomorrow
  - `dak1 haan4 jam2 caa4` (得閒飲茶) - Let's have tea when you're free
  - `baai1 baai3` (拜拜) - Bye bye (Colloquial)
- **Dialogues:**
  - **Dialogue 1 (Formal Closing):**
    - A: `我先走喇。再見。` (我先走喇。再見。 - "I'll be going first. Goodbye.")
    - B: `好，再見。` (好，再見。)
  - **Dialogue 2 (Informal):**
    - A: `阿明，拜拜！得閒飲茶呀！` (阿明，拜拜！得閒飲茶呀！)
    - B: `好呀！聽日見。` (好呀！聽日見。)
- **Exercises:**
  - `2.5.5` (Reading): `再見` (Covers: 再, 見)
  - `2.5.6` (Reading): `聽日見` (Covers: 聽, 日, 見)
  - `2.5.4` (Reading): `拜拜` (Covers: 拜)
  - `2.5.3` (Unscramble): `聽日見` (Covers: 聽, 日, 見)
  - `2.5.2` (Reading): `得閒` (Covers: 得, 閒)
  - `2.5.8` (Reading): `飲茶` (Covers: 飲, 茶)
  - `2.5.7` (Unscramble): `得閒飲茶呀！` (Covers: 得, 閒, 飲, 茶, 呀)
