# Chapter 2: Greetings, Introductions & Small Talk

This chapter covers the essentials of starting and ending conversations in both formal and colloquial settings.

## Lesson 2.1: Hello and "What's Up?"

_Balancing formal greetings with daily colloquialisms._

- **Concepts:** Formal `你好` vs. Spontaneous `點呀?`. Greetings for different times of day.
- **Examples:**
  - `nei5 hou2` (你好) - Hello (Formal)
  - `dim2 aa3?` (點呀？) - What's up? (Colloquial)
  - `zou2 san1` (早晨) - Good morning
  - `ng5 on1` (午安) - Good afternoon (Formal)
  - `maan5 on1` (晚安) - Good evening (Formal)
  - `haa1 lo3` (哈囉) - Hello (Colloquial)
- **Dialogues:**
  - **Dialogue 1 (Formal Morning):**
    - A: `zou2 san1, Wong4 sin1 saang1.` (早晨，黃先生。)
    - B: `zou2 san1, nei5 hou2 maa3?` (早晨，你好嗎？)
    - A: `ngo5 hou2 hou2, jau6 lau4 nei5?` (我好好，有勞你？ - "I'm fine, thanks for asking")
  - **Dialogue 2 (Colloquial):**
    - A: `haa1 lo3! dim2 aa3?` (哈囉！點呀？)
    - B: `gei2 hou2 aa3, nei5 ne1?` (幾好呀，你呢？)
    - A: `ngo5 dou1 hai6 gam2 laa1.` (我都係噉啦。 - "I'm just the same / so-so")
- **Exercises:**
  - `2.1.2` (Reading): `dim2 aa3?`
  - `2.1.3` (Unscramble): `nei5 hou2, zou2 san1`
  - `2.1.4` (Reading): `ng5 on1`

## Lesson 2.2: Who are You?

_Self-introductions and identity._

- **Concepts:** `我係` (I am) vs `我叫` (I am called). Expressing pleasure in meeting.
- **Examples:**
  - `ngo5 hai6` (我係) - I am
  - `ngo5 giu3` (我叫) - My name is
  - `nei5 giu3 me1 meng2?` (你叫咩名？) - What is your name?
  - `hou2 gou1 hing3 sik1 dou2 nei5` (好高興識到你) - Nice to meet you
- **Dialogues:**
  - **Dialogue 1 (Basic Intro):**
    - A: `nei5 hou2! ngo5 giu3 Aa3 Ming4.` (你好！我叫阿明。)
    - B: `Aa3 Ming4, nei5 hou2. ngo5 hai6 Aa3 Ling4.` (阿明，你好。我係阿玲。)
  - **Dialogue 2 (Inquiring Name):**
    - A: `nei5 hou2! nei5 giu3 me1 meng2 aa3?` (你好！你叫咩名呀？)
    - B: `ngo5 giu3 Aa3 Keong4. nei5 ne1?` (我叫阿強。你呢？)
    - A: `ngo5 hai6 Aa3 Fan1. hou2 gou1 hing3 sik1 dou2 nei5.` (我係阿芬。好高興識到你。)
- **Exercises:**
  - `2.2.2` (Reading): `ngo5 giu3 Aa3 Ming4`
  - `2.2.3` (Unscramble): `nei5 giu3 me1 meng2?`
  - `2.2.4` (Reading): `hou2 gou1 hing3 sik1 dou2 nei5`

## Lesson 2.3: The Cultural "Small Talk"

_Introducing the most common Cantonese colloquialisms._

- **Concepts:** Asking about eating as a greeting (`食咗飯未？`) and "Where are you going?" (`去邊呀？`).
- **Examples:**
  - `sik6 zo2 faan6 mei6?` (食咗飯未？) - Have you eaten yet? (Common greeting)
  - `sik6 zo2 laa3` (食咗喇) - Eaten already
  - `heoi3 bin1 aa3?` (去邊呀？) - Where are you going?
- **Dialogue:**
  - A: `Aa3 Ling4! sik6 zo2 faan6 mei6 aa3?` (阿玲！食咗飯未呀？)
  - B: `sik6 zo2 laa3, nei5 ne1?` (食咗喇，你呢？)
  - A: `ngo5 mei6 aa3. ngo5 heoi3 sik6 faan6.` (我未呀。我食飯。 - "I haven't. I'm going to eat.")
- **Exercises:**
  - `2.3.2` (Reading): `sik6 zo2 faan6 mei6?`
  - `2.3.3` (Unscramble): `sik6 zo2 laa3, nei5 ne1?`

## Lesson 2.4: Presenting Others

_Formal and informal introductions of third parties._

- **Concepts:** `呢位係` (This is) and more formal phrases.
- **Examples:**
  - `ni1 wai2 hai6` (呢位係) - This (person) is...
  - `sik1 dou2 nei5 hai6 ngo5 ge3 wing4 hang6` (識到你係我嘅榮幸) - It's my honor to meet you (Formal)
- **Dialogue:**
  - A: `Chan4 sin1 saang1, ni1 wai2 hai6 Wong4 si2 zi2.` (陳先生，呢位係黃小姐。)
  - B: `Wong4 si2 zi2, nei5 hou2. sik1 dou2 nei5 hai6 ngo5 ge3 wing4 hang6.` (黃小姐，你好。識到你係我嘅榮幸。)
  - C: `Chan4 sin1 saang1, nei5 taai3 haak3 hei3 laa3.` (陳先生，你太客氣喇。 - "You're too kind/polite")
- **Exercises:**
  - `2.4.2` (Reading): `ni1 wai2 hai6 Wong4 si2 zi2`
  - `2.4.3` (Unscramble): `sik1 dou2 nei5 hai6 ngo5 ge3 wing4 hang6`

## Lesson 2.5: See You Later & "Yum Cha"

_Closing a conversation and making future plans._

- **Concepts:** Goodbye, "See you tomorrow", and colloquial closings.
- **Examples:**
  - `zoi3 gin3` (再見) - Goodbye (Standard/Formal)
  - `ting1 jat6 gin3` (聽日見) - See you tomorrow
  - `dak1 haan4 jam2 caa4` (得閒飲茶) - Let's have tea when you're free
  - `baai1 baai3` (拜拜) - Bye bye (Colloquial)
- **Dialogues:**
  - **Dialogue 1 (Formal Closing):**
    - A: `Chan4 sin1 saang1, ngo5 sin1 zau2 laa3. zoi3 gin3.` (陳先生，我先走喇。再見。 - "I'll be going first. Goodbye.")
    - B: `hou2, zoi3 gin3.` (好，再見。)
  - **Dialogue 2 (Informal):**
    - A: `Aa3 Ming4, baai1 baai3! dak1 haan4 jam2 caa4 aa3!` (阿明，拜拜！得閒飲茶呀！)
    - B: `hou2 aa3! ting1 jat6 gin3.` (好呀！聽日見。)
- **Exercises:**
  - `2.5.2` (Reading): `dak1 haan4 jam2 caa4`
  - `2.5.3` (Unscramble): `ting1 jat6 gin3`
  - `2.5.4` (Reading): `baai1 baai3`
