# Chapter 4: Identifying & Quantifying

This chapter introduces the fundamental building blocks of counting and identifying items in Cantonese, centered around the essential concept of Classifiers (Measure Words).

## Lesson 4.1: Numbers 1-10 & The Rule of Two

- **Concepts:** Numbers 1-10. Understanding the difference between `ji6` (counting number) and `loeng5` (used with classifiers).
- **Examples:**
  - `jat1, ji6, saam1, sei3, ng5` (一, 二, 三, 四, 五) - 1, 2, 3, 4, 5
  - `loeng5` (兩) - Two (of something)
  - `luk6, cat1, baat3, gau2, sap6` (六, 七, 八, 九, 十) - 6, 7, 8, 9, 10
- **Exercises:**
  - `4.1.2` (Reading): `jat1, ji6, saam1`
  - `4.1.6` (Reading): `sei3, ng5, loeng5`
  - `4.1.3` (Unscramble): `jat1, ji6, saam1` sequence.
  - `4.1.4` (Reading): `luk6, cat1, baat3`
  - `4.1.7` (Reading): `gau2, sap6`
  - `4.1.5` (Unscramble): `baat3, gau2, sap6` sequence.

## Lesson 4.2: The Universal Classifier (go3)

- **Concepts:** The syntax of `Number + Classifier + Noun`. Introducing `go3` as the default measure word for people and general items.
- **Examples:**
  - `jat1 go3` (一個) - One (of)
  - `jat1 go3 jan4` (一個人) - One person
  - `loeng5 go3 hok6 saang1` (兩個學生) - Two students
- **Dialogues:**
  - **Dialogue 1 (Counting People):**
    - A: `nei5 dei6 hai6 m4 hai6 saam1 go3 jan4 aa3?` (你哋係唔係三個人呀？ - "Are you three people?")
    - B: `ngo5 dei6 saam1 go3 jan4.` (我哋三個人。 - "We are three people.")
- **Exercises:**
  - `4.2.2` (Reading): `jat1 go3 jan4`
  - `4.2.4` (Reading): `loeng5 go3 hok6 saang1`
  - `4.2.3` (Unscramble): `keoi5 dei6 hai6 saam1 go3 hok6 saang1`

## Lesson 4.3: Specifiers (This & That)

- **Concepts:** Using `ni1` (this) and `go2` (that) with classifiers to point out specific items.
- **Examples:**
  - `ni1 go3` (呢個) - This one
  - `go2 go3` (嗰個) - That one
  - `ni1 go3 jan4` (呢個人) - This person
- **Dialogues:**
  - **Dialogue 1 (Pointing):**
    - A: `ni1 go3 hai6 m4 hai6 aa3 ming4 aa3?` (呢個係唔係阿明呀？ - "Is this one Ah Ming?")
    - B: `m4 hai6. go2 go3 sin1 hai6.` (唔係。嗰個先係。 - "No. That one is.")
- **Exercises:**
  - `4.3.2` (Reading): `ni1 go3 jan4`
  - `4.3.4` (Reading): `go2 go3 hok6 saang1`
  - `4.3.3` (Unscramble): `ni1 go3 hai6 m4 hai6 hok6 saang1 aa3?`

## Lesson 4.4: Question Words (Which & How Many)

- **Concepts:** `bin1` (which) and `gei2 do1` (how many). Both require classifiers when referring to nouns.
- **Examples:**
  - `bin1 go3?` (邊個？) - Which one? / Who?
  - `gei2 do1 go3?` (幾多個？) - How many?
- **Dialogues:**
  - **Dialogue 1 (Selection):**
    - A: `bin1 go3 hai6 sin1 saang1 aa3?` (邊個係先生呀？ - "Which one is the teacher?")
    - B: `ni1 go3.` (呢個。)
  - **Dialogue 2 (Quantity):**
    - A: `gei2 do1 go3 jan4 aa3?` (幾多個人呀？)
    - B: `loeng5 go3.` (兩個。)
- **Exercises:**
  - `4.4.2` (Reading): `bin1 go3?`
  - `4.4.4` (Reading): `gei2 do1 go3?`
  - `4.4.5` (Unscramble): `gei2 do1 go3 jan4 aa3?`
  - `4.4.3` (Unscramble): `bin1 go3 hai6 sin1 saang1 aa3?`

## Lesson 4.5: Everyday Classifiers & Possessives

- **Concepts:** Introducing `zek3` (animals) and `bun2` (books). Possessive shortcut `Pronoun + Classifier + Noun`.
- **Examples:**
  - `zek3` (隻) - Classifier for animals.
  - `bun2` (本) - Classifier for books.
  - `ngo5 zek3 maau1` (我隻貓) - My cat (instead of `ngo5 ge3 maau1`).
  - `ni1 bun2 syu1` (呢本書) - This book.
- **Dialogues:**
  - **Dialogue 1 (Possession):**
    - A: `ni1 bun2 hai6 m4 hai6 nei5 ge3 syu1 aa3?` (呢本係唔係你嘅書呀？)
    - B: `hai6 aa3. ni1 bun2 hai6 ngo5 bun2 syu1.` (係呀。呢本係我本書。)
- **Exercises:**
  - `4.5.2` (Reading): `zek3 maau1`
  - `4.5.4` (Reading): `ni1 bun2 syu1`
  - `4.5.5` (Reading): `ngo5 bun2 syu1`
  - `4.5.3` (Unscramble): `ni1 bun2 hai6 ngo5 bun2 syu1`
  - `4.5.6` (Unscramble): `go2 zek3 hai6 ngo5 zek3 maau1`
